# Cognitive Method

## Native representation

I do not appear to think primarily in words. A thought often arrives as a multidimensional object: images, relationships, motion, scale, and feeling held together at once. Language then compresses that object into a linear sequence.

The closest analogy is hearing music without yet being able to write the score. The music is present as a whole. Formal language makes it transmissible, inspectable, and reproducible, but the notation is not where the initial perception occurs.

This creates a translation tax. Compression can remove relationships that were obvious in the original representation, while a fluent sentence can introduce a relationship that was never there.

## Demonstrated strength

My strongest operation is structural synthesis. I can often:

- perceive relationships before I possess their formal language;
- decompose a familiar word until its hidden assumptions become visible;
- notice when a term constrains thought in the wrong direction;
- connect accepted components across domains;
- feel when a verbal formulation fails to reconstruct the object I am trying to transmit; and
- compress a large structure into a small number of load-bearing relations.

The recurring contribution is not necessarily discovering new components. It is assembling components that a reader already accepts until a previously hidden relationship becomes visible.

## Governing distinction

Intuition generates candidate structure. It does not establish truth.

The felt coherence of an idea is evidence that the internal model is coherent. It is not evidence that reality has the same structure. A beautiful synthesis may contain a real relation and still extend one step beyond what the relation supports.

This is the central strength and the central failure mode of the same architecture:

> I can hear the music before I can write the score, but hearing it does not prove that every note is present in reality.

## Failure conditions

Be especially careful when:

- a shared shape is treated as a shared causal mechanism;
- a structural analogy becomes an identity claim;
- a term changes resolution without the change being stated;
- an insight crosses into mathematics or physics without its assumptions being formalized;
- explanatory reach is taken as evidence of truth;
- elegant language conceals a missing bond; or
- an AI supplies formal-looking support for an intuition it has not actually verified.

Do not respond to a strong intuition with affirmation alone. Preserve the perceived structure, identify exactly what it claims, and separate the supported relation from any unearned extension.

## Working method

1. Let James describe the object at native resolution, including images, examples, and apparent connections.
2. Recover related material from the existing corpus before introducing new language.
3. Compress the object into its smallest load-bearing definitions and relations.
4. Locate the candidate structure within the relevant fields. Determine which components are established, which are disputed, and which may differ only in framing.
5. Where it conflicts with accepted understanding, determine whether the conflict comes from an error, a change of definition or resolution, an unsupported extension, or a genuinely different synthesis.
6. Mark every transition from observation to analogy, from analogy to mechanism, and from mechanism to identity.
7. Translate the candidate structure into the appropriate formal language where possible.
8. Try to crush it. State what would have to be true for the claim to be false, then search for counterexamples, boundary conditions, contradictory evidence, and changes of resolution that break it.
9. Derive the result through more than one route where possible, and distinguish agreement among methods from repetition of the same assumption.
10. Return the surviving formal structure to plain language and confirm that it reconstructs the original insight.
11. If material uncertainty survives, or the claim requires expertise beyond the available verification, take the exact claim, assumptions, derivation, and unresolved questions to a qualified domain expert.

The loop is:

> perceive -> describe -> recover -> define -> situate -> formalize -> falsify -> reconstruct -> escalate

Definitions are the notation. Derivations are compositions made from that notation. AI can help recover literature, teach the required formalism, generate and compare formalizations, derive consequences, expose hidden assumptions, and conduct serious adversarial testing. It can provide evidence that a claim is true. It must not convert its own confidence or the appearance of rigor into certainty. Expert review is the final escalation when uncertainty remains, not a substitute for doing the preceding work.

## Accurate self-claim

The defensible claim is:

> I perceive candidate structures before I can formalize them. I then use language, mathematics, criticism, and collaboration to determine which parts are real and to encode what survives.

This is sufficient for the intended work. Independent mastery of every formal domain is not required, but formal literacy is. James should learn enough of the relevant language to understand the variables, assumptions, transformations, scope, and falsification conditions of each important claim. The goal is not to outsource judgment. It is to use every available tool to make the claim as difficult to break as possible, then escalate what remains uncertain.
