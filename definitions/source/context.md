# Context

**Context:** Information outside a representation that supplies the precision the representation does not contain.

A word establishes a region of possible meaning, while context locates the intended meaning within it. The word *fire* does not change across these sentences:

> The fire warmed the room.

> The company decided to fire him.

> The soldiers opened fire.

The surrounding information makes a hearth, a dismissal, and gunfire appear from the same symbol. Context does not alter the representation. It constrains what the representation can mean.

The relationship is general. Every symbol must carry its own precision or borrow it from context. The less precision a symbol contains, the more contextual information the receiver must supply to recover the intended meaning. Whatever the symbol leaves unresolved must be resolved elsewhere.
