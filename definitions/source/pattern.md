# Pattern

**Pattern:** A recurring relationship among events or forms produced by an invariant within the mechanism generating them.

A pattern exists before anyone recognizes it. Waves crossed oceans before a mind named their recurrence, and petals grew in repeated relation to a center before anyone described radial symmetry. The observer detects the pattern. The mechanism produces it.

Individual expressions do not need to be identical because what recurs is the relationship. One wave differs from the next, while both are generated by the same governing behavior of a fluid under gravity, pressure, and local conditions. A sequence can contain no repeated number and still preserve the same relationship between successive terms.

Pattern recognition begins with the output. It links repeated observations and allows the relationship to be extrapolated, but that extrapolation silently assumes that the generator and its relevant conditions will remain stable. Understanding moves in the opposite direction: it reconstructs the mechanism, identifies the conditions required for recurrence, and therefore explains both why the pattern continues and where it must fail.

An observer can record a long sequence of sunrises without implying that the sequence will continue forever. Recurrence supports the next prediction. Understanding rotation, orbit, and stellar evolution explains the recurrence while also revealing its boundary.

The governing distinction is:

> A pattern says what has repeated. A mechanism says what must remain true for it to repeat.

A visible resemblance does not establish that its proposed mechanism is correct. The mechanism must be traced independently in each case. When the same causal structure survives across different instances or substrates, the shared pattern is repeated output from repeated causation rather than analogy.
