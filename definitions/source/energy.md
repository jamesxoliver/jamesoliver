# Energy

**Energy:** The common quantity in which physical states and transformations are denominated.

Mass, motion, radiation, heat, and fields are physically different, just as inventory, machinery, and cash are different assets. Assigning each an energy value allows their transformations to enter a common account.

When a physical system changes, energy may move across the system boundary or appear under a different term in the account. Where the relevant laws are invariant through time, the total remains conserved. The entries change while the ledger balances.

Energy is therefore not a substance from which reality is made. It is a quantitative property assigned to physical states. Two states can contain the same energy while differing in configuration, position, momentum, charge, and structure, just as two companies with equal asset values can hold entirely different assets.

The denomination is part of our representation. The invariant relationships that make the account balance belong to reality.
