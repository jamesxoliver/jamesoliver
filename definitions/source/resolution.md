# Resolution

**Resolution:** The unit at which differences within a system become distinguishable.

Resolution partitions a system into units and treats differences within each unit as equivalent. At one resolution, a solar system is a single unit. At a finer resolution, it separates into planets, and each planet can separate into regions, organisms, cells, molecules, and atoms.

Scale determines how much of the system is inside the frame. Resolution determines the smallest unit distinguished within it. A map can show the whole Earth at country resolution or one city at street resolution. Scale is the canvas. Resolution is the size of its pixels.

Coarser resolution combines configurations that finer resolution keeps separate. The territory does not change, but the number of differences preserved by its observation or representation does. Increasing resolution therefore refines the distinguishable possibility space within the description and raises the maximum information the representation can contain.

Language also operates at a chosen resolution. The word *dog* treats many different animals as equivalent. *Golden retriever* preserves more differences, while a name combined with sufficient context can identify one particular dog. Communication succeeds when the units used by one mind reconstruct sufficiently similar boundaries in another.
