# Belief

**Belief:** A representation that a mind takes to correspond to reality.

A mind can imagine, consider, or communicate a representation without believing it. The representation becomes a belief when the mind takes it to correspond to reality. Belief is therefore the mind's relationship to a representation, not evidence that the representation is true.

Belief may be conscious or revealed through expectation and action. A person may say that a bridge is unsafe while stepping onto it without hesitation. The representation governing the step is the operative belief.

A belief may include uncertainty. Someone can believe there is a chance of rain without treating rain as certain. What the mind takes to correspond is the probability represented by the belief.

Reality determines whether the representation is true and how much error it contains.
