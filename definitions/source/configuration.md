# Configuration

**Configuration:** One specific arrangement of the units within a system.

A shuffled deck has 52! possible configurations, but the deck in your hand occupies exactly one. Every card has one position, and the complete arrangement of cards across those positions is the configuration.

A snowflake is likewise one configuration of its constituent units. Another snowflake may be produced by the same generative mechanism and preserve the same sixfold pattern, but it occupies a different configuration because its specific arrangement differs.

Resolution determines the units and which differences count. Two arrangements that appear identical at visible resolution may be different configurations at molecular resolution.

A configuration is one point within a possibility space:

> x ∈ Ω

A region contains multiple configurations that share specified properties. “The four aces are on top” defines a region containing every exact deck arrangement compatible with that constraint. Naming the position of every card reduces that region to one configuration.

At a given resolution and moment, a system occupies one configuration within the chosen description. Its possibility space contains the configurations it could occupy under that description.
