# Medium

**Medium:** A physical substrate whose distinguishable states carry an encoded pattern.

Information does not travel or persist in the abstract. It must be instantiated as a difference that some system can distinguish: pressure in air, marks on paper, displaced stone, electrical charge, magnetic orientation, molecular sequence, or another physical state.

The same abstract pattern can move through several media. A thought becomes pressure waves in speech, electrical states in a recording system, changing air pressure during playback, and neural activity in a listener. The medium changes while enough of the relationship among states survives for the pattern to be reconstructed.

A medium influences how far an encoding can travel, how long it remains recoverable, how easily it can be copied, and what machinery is required to interpret it. These are properties of the carrier rather than the information considered in isolation.
