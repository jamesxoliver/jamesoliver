# Encoding

**Encoding:** The conversion of information into distinguishable physical states of a medium.

The encoded states need not resemble what they carry. What matters is that differences in the source produce differences in the medium that a compatible system can distinguish. Encoding therefore requires information, a medium, and a rule connecting states of one to states of the other.

Speech encodes language into transient pressure patterns. Writing encodes language into comparatively persistent graphic or tactile patterns. An audio system converts sound into physical states from which the sound can be regenerated. DNA encodes biological instructions into molecular sequence.

Encoding does not necessarily compress. Information may be translated without loss within a defined possibility space. Compression is one possible property of an encoding, not the operation itself.
