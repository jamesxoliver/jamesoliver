# Mass

**Mass:** The frame-invariant magnitude of a system's combined energy and momentum.

The energy and momentum assigned to a system depend on the observer's frame. Mass is the relationship between them that does not:

\[
m^2c^4 = E^2 - p^2c^2
\]

In the system's center-of-momentum frame, its total momentum is zero and the relationship reduces to \(E_0 = mc^2\). Zero total momentum does not mean that nothing inside the system is moving. Internal motion, heat, radiation, and binding all contribute to the mass of the whole.

A sealed box containing equal beams of light traveling in opposite directions has zero total momentum and positive mass, even though each photon is massless and never at rest. Their momenta cancel while their energies remain.

Mass is therefore not matter made solid or energy without motion. It is the invariant preserved across different representations of the same system's energy and momentum: what observers can agree upon while disagreeing about how the system is moving.
