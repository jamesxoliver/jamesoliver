# Word

**Word:** A symbolic boundary drawn around a region of possible meaning so that the region can be treated as a transmissible unit.

A word acts like a lasso. It gathers different experiences inside one boundary and gives them a common symbol. The boundary distinguishes what lies outside the word by compressing differences among what lies within it.

The larger the enclosed region, the more interpretations the word admits and the less precisely it identifies any one of them. *Love* can refer to attachment, desire, care, loyalty, sacrifice, affection, and many other configurations. The numeral *1*, within a specified mathematical system, occupies a much narrower region because its relationships to other numbers are formally constrained.

The scope-precision relationship is:

> A word's precision decreases as the number of plausible interpretations it admits increases.

Context can narrow the region, but the word alone cannot contain precision it does not carry. A broad word gains reach by surrendering resolution.
