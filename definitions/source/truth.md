# Truth

**Truth:** An assertive representation with zero error within the conditions and resolution of its claim.

Reality simply exists. Truth appears only when a representation asserts that reality is a particular way. The territory is reality. The map is the representation. The map is true when what it asserts corresponds to the territory within the boundary of its claim.

Not every representation is true or false. A melody or imagined creature can represent without asserting that reality is a particular way. Truth applies only where a representation makes a claim that reality can answer.

The boundary is part of the claim. *The Earth is round* is true during the interval in which Earth exists in an approximately spherical form and at the resolution where mountains and valleys do not determine the represented shape. Before Earth formed, the claim had no object. After Earth ceases to exist, it will again have no object. The sentence appears timeless only because its coordinates have been omitted.

Different representations of the same territory can therefore be true at the same time. A transit map may preserve station order while distorting physical distance. It is true as a representation of sequence and false if treated as a representation of geography. A representation must be evaluated only against what it claims to preserve.

Every complete empirical truth contains spatial, temporal, and resolution bounds. Logical and mathematical truths may not depend on a particular region of space or time, but they still operate within the conditions and resolution of their claims. Truth is binary because the claim determines which differences matter. *The Earth is approximately spherical* is true at the resolution stated by the claim, while *the Earth is a perfect sphere* is false. The approximation belongs to the claim rather than weakening its truth.

This definition fails if something can meaningfully be true without asserting anything, or if an assertion can remain true while differing from reality within the conditions and resolution of its claim.
