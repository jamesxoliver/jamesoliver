# System

**System:** A set of components connected by relationships through which change can propagate.

Interaction distinguishes a system from an arbitrary collection. When the state of one component can alter the state of another, change can move through their relationship and affect the configuration of the whole.

A system does not require a purpose. Engines and companies may be organized toward stated ends, while weather systems, ecosystems, and solar systems arise without one. What they share is not intention but interaction.

The boundary of a system depends on the resolution and question. A cell is a system when its organelles and molecular processes are under examination, and the same cell becomes one component when the system is a tissue. A system at one resolution can become a component at another.

Systems can also overlap. One person can participate simultaneously in a marriage, family, company, friendship, and political system. Husband, brother, employer, friend, and citizen are not isolated properties of the person. They are functions produced by different relationships.

A system is therefore defined by interaction rather than exclusive membership or physical containment.
