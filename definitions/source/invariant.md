# Invariant

**Invariant:** What remains the same while other things change.

An invariant can only be identified across variation. Several configurations differ in position, form, scale, symbols, or time, while preserving some quantity, relationship, or structure. That preserved sameness is the invariant.

Rotate a square and its position changes while its side lengths and angles remain. Change inertial reference frames and a system's measured energy and momentum change while its mass remains. The changing expressions reveal what they share.

Nothing is invariant without a boundary. A property may remain unchanged under rotation and change under scaling. A mechanism may persist across locations and fail when its conditions change. An invariant is therefore always relative to the differences across which it has been preserved.
