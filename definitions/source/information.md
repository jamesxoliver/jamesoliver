# Information

**Information:** A reduction in possibility produced by constraint.

A constraint describes the boundary imposed. Information describes the reduction that follows. More information leaves fewer possible configurations, while less information leaves more.

A cookie cutter makes the relationship visible. The dough can take many shapes before the cutter is imposed. The cutter eliminates every shape outside its boundary, leaving a smaller set of configurations available to the dough. The cutter is the constraint. The possibility it eliminates is the information.

This relationship can be physical or observational. A physical constraint reduces what a system can do. An observation reduces what an observer must consider possible. Learning that an object is a fruit leaves many possibilities. Learning that it is a small green apple eliminates most of them even though the object itself has not changed.

Information must therefore be defined relative to a possibility space. A string of symbols may precisely constrain which symbols were transmitted while providing no constraint on what they mean. Adding symbols does not necessarily add information because redundant or unusable symbols may eliminate no additional possibilities.

The amount of information is the effective reduction in possibility, not the number of symbols or constraints used to produce it. Let Ω be the original possibility space and let C be the constraint-compatible region that remains, with C ⊆ Ω. The eliminated region is E = Ω ∖ C, so:

> Ω = C ∪ E

In the uniform case, the information imposed by the constraint is:

> *I*(Ω, C) = log₂(μ(Ω) / μ(C))

If C = Ω, no possibility has been eliminated and the information added is zero. If C is smaller than Ω, the information is positive. If C is empty, the constraints are incompatible because no configuration satisfies them together.

Within a uniform possibility space at a specified resolution, entropy is the logarithmic measure of the possibility that remains, while information gained is the logarithmic reduction from the prior space.
