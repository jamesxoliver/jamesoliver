# Possibility Space

**Possibility space:** The configurations that remain distinguishable and available under a given set of constraints.

A possibility space is never simply everything that could happen. Its boundary depends on the system being considered, the variables being tracked, the constraints already accepted, and the resolution at which differences are distinguished.

A map makes the structure visible. A particular address can be located through a sequence such as Earth, United States, New York, New York City, street, building, and apartment. Each level inherits the constraints above it and adds another boundary, eliminating locations that were possible at the previous level.

Let Ω₀ be a wider possibility space. A constraint produces a compatible subset Ω₁, which can then become the possibility space upon which the next constraint acts:

> Ω₀ ⊃ Ω₁ ⊃ Ω₂ ⊃ ··· ⊃ Ωₙ

What appears unconstrained at one level is therefore already constrained by the frame used to define it. Every operational possibility space is a constrained region within a larger one.

Resolution determines which configurations count as different. At continental resolution, every location within a continent is treated as equivalent. At apartment resolution, those locations become distinguishable. Increasing resolution separates states that were previously grouped together, while decreasing resolution compresses them into the same state.

There is no usable space of all possibilities without a frame. There are only possibility spaces defined by prior constraints and distinguished at a particular resolution.
