# Definitions Source

This directory is the private local source for Definitions. An entry is added after James considers its functional definition complete. Entries may continue to change as deeper decomposition exposes a false boundary or a more general mechanism.

Nothing in this directory is published automatically. Publication requires a separate, explicit decision.

This corpus is a parallel successor to Khazadum, not an automatic rewrite of it. Search Khazadum to recover James's prior thinking, but preserve it as the compiled record of the existing framework unless James explicitly requests an edit there. New Derivations built from these Definitions live under `derivations/source/` in this repository.

The collection is alphabetical. It has no required semantic hierarchy. A term earns an entry when its ordinary meaning hides a function James has actually seen.

## Governing Resolution

Define each thing at the simplest resolution that still preserves what makes it distinct.

The thing being defined does not need to be primitive, but its definition should terminate at primitive operations. Decompose until removing another distinction would make the definition less true or make it describe something else, then stop. A coarser definition collapses the thing into adjacent things. A finer definition adds detail without increasing understanding.

Do not substitute surface category for function, and do not reduce everything to its physical substrate. The target is the lowest explanatory layer that still preserves identity: the simplest operation from which the familiar thing can be reconstructed.

## Definitions and Derivations

Definitions are the atoms of the public body of work. Each Definition isolates one reusable conceptual ingredient and stabilizes it independently at the simplest resolution that preserves its identity. Its meaning should remain intact wherever it is later used.

Derivations are molecules. They combine completed Definitions in a particular logical or causal configuration and make visible what follows from their relationship. The contribution often does not reside in any ingredient separately. It appears when accepted ingredients are brought into contact: the reader already accepts A, B, and C; the Derivation shows what happens when they touch.

Do not manufacture papers to populate the site, and do not enumerate combinations merely because they are available. Continue building the inventory until a real question or observed connection supplies the constraints that select relevant Definitions. A Derivation is ready when its load-bearing terms have stable Definitions and the bonds among them can be demonstrated rather than asserted. Test each bond for logical or causal dependence so that resemblance is not mistaken for mechanism.

The same Definition may participate in many Derivations, just as one atom may participate in many molecules or one ingredient in many dishes. Reuse is the point. If a term must change meaning to make a Derivation work, either the Definition is unstable, the paper needs a different term, or the proposed bond is false.

Existing papers can be audited by decomposing them into their Definitions, locating any unstated or unstable ingredient, and repairing the specific bond that fails. The lexicon is therefore the reusable intellectual inventory; the papers are the useful configurations produced from it.

Before work begins on any Definition, search Khazadum for the term, synonyms, implied mechanisms, and earlier names. Search the compiled layers first, then recover raw material where useful. Separate current understanding from superseded drafts before using the recovered material as the starting point for decomposition.
